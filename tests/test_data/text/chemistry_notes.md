# NEET Chemistry - Organic Chemistry Notes

## Unit 1: Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry

### Mole Concept
- **Mole** is the amount of substance containing Avogadro's number (6.022 × 10²³) of particles.
- **Molar mass** is the mass of one mole of a substance.

### Important Formulas
- Number of moles = Mass / Molar mass
- Number of moles = Volume (L) / 22.4 L (at STP)
- Molarity = Number of moles / Volume (L)

## Unit 2: Structure of Atom

### Atomic Models
1. **Thomson's Model** - Plum pudding model
2. **Rutherford's Model** - Nuclear model
3. **Bohr's Model** - Quantized energy levels

### Quantum Numbers
- **Principal quantum number (n)**: Shell number
- **Azimuthal quantum number (l)**: Subshell (s, p, d, f)
- **Magnetic quantum number (m)**: Orbital orientation
- **Spin quantum number (s)**: Electron spin (+½ or -½)

### Electronic Configuration
Rules for electron configuration:
1. Aufbau principle
2. Pauli exclusion principle
3. Hund's rule of maximum multiplicity

## Unit 3: Classification of Elements

### Modern Periodic Table
- 18 groups (vertical columns)
- 7 periods (horizontal rows)
- Elements arranged in increasing atomic number

### Periodic Trends
1. **Atomic size**: Decreases across a period, increases down a group
2. **Ionization energy**: Increases across a period
3. **Electron affinity**: Increases across a period
4. **Electronegativity**: Increases across a period

## Unit 4: Chemical Bonding

### Types of Bonds
1. **Ionic bond**: Transfer of electrons
2. **Covalent bond**: Sharing of electrons
3. **Metallic bond**: Delocalized electrons
4. **Hydrogen bond**: Intermolecular attraction

### VSEPR Theory
- Electron pairs repel each other
- Geometry depends on number of electron pairs
- Bond angle varies with hybridization

### Hybridization
| Type | Geometry | Bond Angle |
|------|----------|------------|
| sp | Linear | 180° |
| sp² | Trigonal planar | 120° |
| sp³ | Tetrahedral | 109.5° |
| sp³d | Trigonal bipyramidal | 90°, 120° |
| sp³d² | Octahedral | 90° |

## Unit 5: States of Matter

### Gas Laws
1. **Boyle's Law**: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ (constant T)
2. **Charles's Law**: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ (constant P)
3. **Avogadro's Law**: V₁/n₁ = V₂/n₂ (constant T, P)

### Ideal Gas Equation
PV = nRT

### Graham's Law of Diffusion
Rate of diffusion ∝ 1/√M

## Unit 6: Thermodynamics

### Laws of Thermodynamics
- **First Law**: ΔU = q + w
- **Second Law**: ΔS universe > 0
- **Third Law**: Absolute zero cannot be reached

### Enthalpy
- ΔH = ΔU + Δn_g RT
- ΔH for reaction = Σ ΔH(products) - Σ ΔH(reactants)

## Unit 7: Equilibrium

### Le Chatelier's Principle
When a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts to counteract the change.

### Factors Affecting Equilibrium
1. Concentration
2. Temperature
3. Pressure (for gaseous reactions)
4. Catalyst (no effect)

## Unit 8: Redox Reactions

### Oxidation and Reduction
- **Oxidation**: Loss of electrons, increase in oxidation number
- **Reduction**: Gain of electrons, decrease in oxidation number

### Balancing Redox Reactions
1. Oxidation number method
2. Ion-electron method
