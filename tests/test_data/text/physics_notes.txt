# NEET Physics - Kinematics Notes

## Chapter 1: Motion in One Dimension

### Distance and Displacement
- **Distance** is the total path length traveled by an object.
- **Displacement** is the shortest distance between initial and final positions.

### Speed and Velocity
- **Average Speed** = Total Distance / Total Time
- **Average Velocity** = Total Displacement / Total Time

### Equations of Motion (Uniform Acceleration)
For a body moving with uniform acceleration:
1. v = u + at
2. s = ut + 1/2 at²
3. v² = u² + 2as

Where:
- u = initial velocity
- v = final velocity
- a = acceleration
- t = time
- s = displacement

## Chapter 2: Motion in Two Dimensions

### Projectile Motion
A projectile motion has two components:
1. Horizontal component (constant velocity)
2. Vertical component (uniform acceleration due to gravity)

### Equations for Projectile Motion
- Time of flight: T = 2u sinθ / g
- Maximum height: H = u² sin²θ / 2g
- Range: R = u² sin2θ / g

### Key Points
- For maximum range, θ = 45°
- For maximum height, θ = 90°
- At maximum height, vertical velocity = 0

## Chapter 3: Laws of Motion

### Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia)
A body continues in its state of rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

### Newton's Second Law
Force = mass × acceleration
F = ma

### Newton's Third Law
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

### Friction
- Static friction: fs ≤ μs N
- Kinetic friction: fk = μk N
- μs > μk (static friction > kinetic friction)

## Chapter 4: Work, Energy and Power

### Work
Work = Force × Displacement × cosθ
W = F × s × cosθ

### Kinetic Energy
KE = 1/2 mv²

### Potential Energy
PE = mgh

### Work-Energy Theorem
Work done = Change in kinetic energy

### Conservation of Energy
Total mechanical energy remains constant in the absence of non-conservative forces.

### Power
Power = Work / Time
P = W / t = F × v

## Chapter 5: Rotational Motion

### Angular Velocity
ω = θ / t
v = rω

### Moment of Inertia
I = Σ mr²

### Torque
τ = I × α
τ = r × F

### Angular Momentum
L = I × ω
