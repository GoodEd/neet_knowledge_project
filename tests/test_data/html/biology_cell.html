NEET Biology: Cell - The Unit of Life

1. Introduction to Cell Biology

The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms. All cells arise from pre-existing cells through cell division.

Key Point: Cell theory was proposed by Schleiden and Schwann in 1839.

2. Structure of Cell

2.1 Prokaryotic Cells

Prokaryotic cells are simple cells without a nucleus. Examples: Bacteria, Blue-green algae.

  • Size: 0.1 - 5.0 micrometer
  • No well-defined nucleus
  • DNA is circular and not associated with histones
  • Cell wall is present (peptidoglycan)
  • No membrane-bound organelles

2.2 Eukaryotic Cells

Eukaryotic cells have a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Examples: Plant and Animal cells.

  • Size: 10 - 100 micrometer
  • Well-defined nucleus
  • DNA is linear and associated with histones
  • Cell wall present in plant cells (cellulose)
  • Membrane-bound organelles present

3. Cell Organelles

Organelle Function
Nucleus Control center, contains genetic material
Mitochondria Powerhouse of cell, ATP production
Ribosomes Protein synthesis
Endoplasmic Reticulum Protein and lipid synthesis
Golgi Apparatus Packaging and secretion
Lysosomes Digestion, autophagy
Chloroplast Photosynthesis (plant cells)

4. Cell Membrane (Plasma Membrane)

4.1 Structure - Fluid Mosaic Model

Proposed by Singer and Nicolson (1972). The membrane is composed of:

  • Phospholipid bilayer
  • Proteins (integral and peripheral)
  • Cholesterol
  • Carbohydrates (glycolipids and glycoproteins)

4.2 Functions

  1. Selective permeability
  2. Cell recognition
  3. Transport of materials
  4. Receptor function

5. Cell Division

5.1 Mitosis

Equational division. Produces two identical daughter cells.

Stages: Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase

5.2 Meiosis

Reductional division. Produces four haploid daughter cells.

Stages: Prophase I → Metaphase I → Anaphase I → Telophase I → Prophase II → Metaphase II → Anaphase II → Telophase II

NEET Important: Crossing over occurs in Prophase I of meiosis, leading to genetic variation.
